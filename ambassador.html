Global Heart Equity Institute (GHEI)

Building a global community for equitable heart health

What is GHEI?

The Global Heart Equity Institute (GHEI) is an international organization dedicated to improving cardiovascular health equity through education, community engagement, and collaborative global partnerships. Our vision is a world where heart-health knowledge and resources are accessible regardless of geography, language, or socioeconomic status.

Why We Do This

Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death worldwide — yet largely preventable. Disparities in knowledge, access, and prevention leave many communities behind. GHEI aims to close these gaps through culturally grounded communication and global collaboration.

Our Core Focus Areas

1. Health Literacy & Education
Culturally tailored heart-health materials, awareness activities, youth engagement.

2. Community & Country-Based Engagement
Supporting local initiatives, organizing workshops, understanding regional needs.

3. Global Collaboration Network
Linking universities, hospitals, NGOs, and individuals across countries.

4. Small-Scale Research & Pilot Projects
Needs assessments, surveys, feasibility studies (PPG-related pilots included later).

About the Ambassador Program

GHEI Global Ambassadors represent their region and help:

  • Identify local heart-health needs
  • Support small-scale projects and surveys
  • Share cultural and linguistic insights
  • Facilitate partner connections
  • Join global discussions and initiatives

Ambassadors are recognized contributors to GHEI’s global mission.

What Ambassadors Receive

  • Official GHEI Global Ambassador Certificate
  • Participation acknowledgment
  • Listing on the GHEI website
  • Opportunity to join future global projects
  • Possibility of research involvement and acknowledgments

GHEI Global Country Ambassador – Role Description

You can also reuse this in job posts, LinkedIn role descriptions, or internal documentation.

Position Type

Non-paid, flexible, international partnership role.

Purpose

To serve as a representative for your country/region within the GHEI global network, helping identify local needs, connect partners, and support community and collaborative activities.

Key Responsibilities

1. Local Insight & Needs Identification

  • Share perspectives on cardiovascular health challenges in your region
  • Support needs assessments or small-scale surveys when applicable

2. Community & Engagement Support

  • Help localize and share health education content
  • Participate in or organize small-scale awareness activities

3. Partnership Facilitation

  • Connect GHEI with potential collaborators (universities, hospitals, NGOs)
  • Provide cultural or linguistic context for cross-country collaboration

4. Optional Research Involvement

  • Join pilot projects or data collection activities
  • Contribute to country-level feasibility assessments

5. Global Network Participation

  • Join virtual meetings and workshops
  • Share updates or insights with the global team

Time Commitment

Flexible, typically 1–3 hours per week depending on activities.

Benefits for Ambassadors

  • Official GHEI Ambassador Certificate
  • Recognition on the GHEI website
  • Priority access to global projects and workshops
  • Opportunity for acknowledgment in reports and publications
  • Experience in international public health collaboration
